Our Democracy Journey

Nigeria democratized in 1999 with the start of the Fourth Republic, but has suffered some setbacks to becoming fully democratic. Elites in Nigeria have been found to have more power and influence than average citizens, and as a consequence of this, there has been a great deal of corruption in Nigerian politics and general life. A good sign of democracy in Nigeria is the fact that elections are becoming less fraudulent and there is more party competition. Another indicator of a strong democracy is the presence of a civil society in which citizens have the right to act and speak freely in concert with a strong use of media for everyday life. Furthermore, Nigeria has seen a heightened use of media within the realm of political issues, particularly with the recent [Special Anti-Robbery Squad] SARS protest, indicating a sense of freedom for the public to voice their opinions to the government and the world. Nigeria contains more historic cultures and empires than any other nation in Africa. They date back as far as the 5th century BC. The nation's potential remains vast, with at least 216,771,563 as of Wednesday, August 3, 2022, based on World meter elaboration of the latest United Nations data (comprising some 250 tribes) it is the continent's most populous country. Nigeria has lost its status as Africa’s top oil producer to Angola as the continent’s biggest economy saw its output decline the most in May among its peers in the Organization of the Petroleum Exporting Countries (OPEC) Nigerians, once dominated by the military, have become disappointed in the civilian rule initiated in 1999, due to the increasing poverty, ethnic strife, religious intolerance, declining standards in health and education, and a stagnant economy.

A country where Unity and Faith, Peace and Progress is our motto Home to over 200 million inhabitants, Nigeria has one of the largest populations of youth, and is the sixth most populous country in the world

Mission

To create a platform that will enable Nigerians within and in diaspora to support the presidential candidate of their choice in the forth coming 2023 elections.

Vision

To eliminate God-fatherism and raise a neutral leader that will restore the dignity of our dear country and respect the will of the people.

Problem Statement

To eliminate barriers in raising funds for competent presidential candidates more especially for those with less financial backup to project their manifesto to Nigerians globally

Our Solution

BlockChain Nigeria Users' Group, A decentralized Autonomous Organisation, empowers Nigerians worldwide with opportunity to support their preferred presidential candidate through the use of a boundless financial payment system that is decentralized, transparent and immutable.

Action Plan

Let us create a neutral government of the people by the people and for the people.
